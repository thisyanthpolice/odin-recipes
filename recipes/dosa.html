dosa recipe

Description:

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that is widely enjoyed across India and around the world. It is a thin, crispy, and savory pancake made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. The batter is typically ground into a smooth paste and left to ferment overnight, which helps develop its characteristic tangy flavor and airy texture.

Ingredients:

  • rice - 1 cup
  • Urad Dal (Black Gram Lentils) - 1/4 cup
  • Fenugreek Seeds - 1/2 teaspoon (optional)
  • Water - As needed
  • Salt - To taste
  • Oil or Ghee (Clarified Butter) - For greasing the pan

steps:

  1. Soak the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds (if using) in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them into a smooth batter, adding water as needed. The batter should have a thick yet pourable consistency.
  3. Allow the batter to ferment for 8-12 hours or overnight at room temperature. The batter should rise and become slightly tangy.
  4. Once fermented, stir the batter gently and add salt to taste.
  5. Heat a non-stick griddle or tawa over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  6. Pour a ladle of batter onto the hot griddle and spread it into a thin, circular shape.
  7. Cook the dosa on one side until it turns golden brown and crispy. Optionally, drizzle a little more oil or ghee around the edges for added crispiness.
  8. If making masala dosa, prepare the filling by saut√©ing onions, green chilies, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and turmeric. Add boiled and mashed potatoes, and cook until well-mixed. Season with salt and coriander leaves.
  9. If desired, place the masala filling in the center of the dosa, fold it over, and serve.
  10. Serve the dosa with chutneys (such as coconut chutney, tomato chutney) and sambar.
